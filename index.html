Dog Commands

Pro tip: Say their name before the command for best results (e.g., "Aries, come"). Snapping your fingers when giving commands helps get their attention.

Sit / Stay / Come
Classic obedience commands
Leave It
When they try to sniff or eat something they shouldn't
Walk
Get them to slow down/stop running
Go Potty
Use when they're sniffing a spot
Cross
Cross at crosswalk (makes them run - be ready with "walk")
Out
Kick them out of a room (Spanish: "Vete!")
Get Her!
Triggers them to chase Baddie
Thank You
Makes them surrender what's in their mouth
Excuse Me
Makes them move out of the way
Kennel
Sends them to their kennel
No
Can stand alone or be followed by unwanted behavior

Walking & Leash Tips

ü¶Æ Leashes

Both leashes work the same: lockable and extendable.

Press down the button to lock the length.

Sometimes they need reminding to go - saying "Go Potty" usually works.

üö¶ Leash Pulling/Tugging

Most tugging happens:

  • Right after leaving
  • Exiting elevators
  • First steps outside
  • When they see other dogs
  • When someone walks by

How to handle:

  • Use firm voice: "Sit" or "Stop"
  • If that fails: Beep + "No pulling"
  • Always correct every pull for consistency
